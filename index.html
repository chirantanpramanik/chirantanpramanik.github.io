About Me

I am an isotope geochemist working on stable isotope fractionation in carbonate minerals using density functional theory (DFT), ab initio molecular dynamics (AIMD), and experimental geochemistry. My research focuses on Mg, S, Ca, O and clumped isotope systems, carbonate-associated sulfate, impurity effects in minerals, and the geochemical applications of isotope proxies in environmental and sedimentary systems.

Research Interests

  • Stable isotope geochemistry
  • Carbonate minerals (calcite, aragonite, dolomite)
  • Mg, S and Ca isotope fractionation
  • Carbonate-associated sulfate (CAS)
  • Clumped isotope geochemistry
  • Density Functional Theory (DFT)
  • Ab initio Molecular Dynamics (AIMD)
  • Paleoenvironmental and paleoclimate proxies

Current Research

  • Impurity effects on isotope fractionation in calcite and aragonite
  • Equilibrium isotope fractionation factors from first-principles calculations
  • Carbonate-associated sulfate isotope geochemistry
  • Mg and Ca isotope systematics in carbonate minerals
  • Clumped isotope signatures in sulfate-bearing carbonates
